$$\frac{K \cdot \omega_n^2}{s^2 + 2\omega_n d_r s + \omega_n^2}$$

## 2. Majorana Criterion & Voltage Estimation (Middle Section)

* Gyroscopic Physics: The output angle passes through a sin block and is multiplied by the structural gain block:
$$\frac{4 \cdot I_t \cdot m \cdot g \cdot h}{I_s^2}$$ 
* Minimum Spin ($W_s$): Taking the square root (sqrt) of this product yields the minimum required angular velocity ($\omega_s$) to satisfy the Majorana Criterion for gyroscopic stability.
* Voltage Conversion: Multiplying by the back-EMF constant (Ke) converts this target spin speed into the baseline motor Voltage needed.
* Sample & Hold: The discrete relational operators and Sample and Hold blocks lock or discrete-sample this calculated voltage at specific intervals (governed by your Ts = 0.01 sampling period).
